Why this exists

Search "in-hand salary calculator" and count the ad units before you find the input box. Most Indian finance calculators are ad farms with a formula bolted on — and half of them are still running last year's slabs.

Hisaab is the opposite bet: correct numbers, current rules, zero clutter. Every calculator has a show the math panel because a number you can't verify is just a rumour with confidence. The rates live in one config file, cross-checked against official sources and locked with tests, and the whole thing is open source.

Questions people ask

Is anything I type stored or sent anywhere?

No. Every calculator runs entirely in your browser — there is no server, no account, no analytics script, and no cookie. Close the tab and it's gone.

Why should I trust the numbers?

Every formula is cross-checked against at least two independent sources (official portals plus ClearTax, Zerodha and the like) and locked in with automated tests before a page ships. The whole site is open source — you can read the exact formula behind any result.

Old regime or new regime — which is better for me?

Whichever leaves more in your bank for your specific numbers — it mostly depends on how much you actually claim in deductions like HRA, 80C and home-loan interest. The salary calculator computes both regimes side by side so you can see the difference instead of guessing.
