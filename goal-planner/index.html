Why inflate the goal

A goal that costs ₹1 crore today will not cost ₹1 crore in 15 years. At 6% inflation it roughly doubles every 12 years — so planning against today's price systematically undershoots. This planner first inflates today's cost to the goal date, then computes the SIP (instalment at the start of each month, compounding monthly) or the single upfront amount that reaches that inflated target at your expected return.
