SIP or lumpsum — what this page computes

A SIP (systematic investment plan) invests a fixed amount every month, so your money enters the market gradually at many price points. A lumpsum invests everything on day one, so the full amount compounds for the whole tenure. This page computes the arithmetic of both at a constant expected return — it does not tell you which to choose, because the answer depends on whether you already have the lumpsum, market conditions, and your own risk tolerance.

  • At the same total invested and a steadily rising market, the lumpsum compounds longer.
  • A SIP spreads entry prices — useful when investing from a monthly salary.
