How the in-hand salary is calculated

Take-home pay = gross salary − employee EPF − professional tax − income tax. EPF is 12% of Basic + DA. Professional tax is a state levy capped at ₹2,500/year and directly reduces take-home; it is deductible from taxable income only in the old regime.

New regime (default), FY 2026-27

Standard deduction ₹75,000. Slabs: nil up to ₹4L, then 5% (₹4–8L), 10% (₹8–12L), 15% (₹12–16L), 20% (₹16–20L), 25% (₹20–24L), 30% above ₹24L. A Section 87A rebate (up to ₹60,000) makes taxable income up to ₹12,00,000 tax-free — so a salaried person pays no tax up to about ₹12.75 lakh gross. Just above ₹12L, marginal relief caps the tax at the amount by which income exceeds ₹12L. Chapter-VI-A deductions (80C, 80D, HRA, etc.) and professional tax are not allowed here.

Old regime, FY 2026-27

Standard deduction ₹50,000, plus 80C (₹1.5L), 80D, HRA, home-loan interest (₹2L), NPS 80CCD(1B) (₹50k) and more. Slabs: nil up to ₹2.5L (₹3L for age 60–80, ₹5L for 80+), 5% (₹2.5–5L), 20% (₹5–10L), 30% above ₹10L. The 87A rebate (₹12,500) makes taxable income up to ₹5L tax-free.

A 4% health & education cess applies to the tax in both regimes; a surcharge applies at incomes above ₹50 lakh (capped at 25% in the new regime).
