Which cities count as “metro”? Settled metros (50%): Delhi, Mumbai, Kolkata, Chennai. Everywhere else has been 40% (Gurugram/Gurgaon included). For FY 2026-27, reports say the 50% list was widened to also cover Bengaluru, Hyderabad, Pune and Ahmedabad under the new Income-tax Rules, 2026 — but we couldn’t confirm this against the official gazette. If you live in one of these four, verify with the notified rule or a CA before choosing 50%.

How HRA exemption works

Under Section 10(13A), the tax-free part of your HRA is the least of:

  • the actual HRA received;
  • 50% of salary (metro) or 40% (non-metro);
  • rent paid minus 10% of salary.

Here “salary” means Basic + eligible DA + commission as a fixed % of turnover — not your full CTC. The remaining HRA (received minus exempt) is added to taxable salary.

Key conditions

  • Old regime only. HRA exemption is not available under the new (default) regime — there, the entire HRA is taxable.
  • You must actually pay rent and not own the home you live in.
  • Landlord PAN is required if annual rent exceeds ₹1,00,000.
  • Period-wise: if inputs change mid-year, split into periods and add up — different limbs can bind in different months.

For information only — not tax advice. The “metro” city list for the 50% slab is subject to the notified Income-tax Rules; confirm your city’s treatment before relying on it. Consult a qualified professional for your situation.
