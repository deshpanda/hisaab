Show the math

    Model: both sides spend max(owner outflow, rent) monthly; the cheaper side invests the difference at your expected return (monthly compounding). The buyer's upfront outgo is down payment + stamp/registration; the renter instead invests that money (minus the deposit) on day one. Selling costs, home-loan tax breaks and rent HRA benefits are not modelled — they can shift the result.

    What this model counts

    • Buyer: pays down payment + stamp duty upfront, then EMI + maintenance monthly (maintenance escalating annually). Gains: home appreciation and loan paydown. After the loan ends, the buyer's monthly cost drops to maintenance alone — the model then invests the buyer's savings too.
    • Renter: pays rent (escalating annually) and a refundable deposit, and invests everything the buyer would have spent extra — starting with the down payment on day one.
    • Not modelled: Section 24(b)/80C tax breaks on the loan, HRA benefit on rent, brokerage on sale, vacancy or society transfer charges. List your own — the point of the model is that every assumption is visible and editable.

    For information only — not financial advice. Housing decisions involve much more than arithmetic (stability, mobility, family). This page computes net worth under your assumptions; it does not tell you what to do.
